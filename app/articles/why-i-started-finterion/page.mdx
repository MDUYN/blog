# Why I Started Finterion

<div className="text-sm text-[var(--color-terminal-text-dim)] mt-2 mb-8">
  January 2022
</div>
</div>

Algorithmic trading has been around for decades. Hedge funds, investment banks, and institutional investors have used it to analyze markets around the clock, execute trades with precision, and remove emotional bias from their decisions. But for everyone else? It's been locked behind proprietary systems, six-figure minimums, and teams of PhDs.

I started [Finterion](https://finterion.com) because I think that's wrong.

## The problem I kept seeing

I've been building trading algorithms and open-source tools for years. I created the [Investing Algorithm Framework](https://github.com/coding-kitties/investing-algorithm-framework) to make it easier for developers to build and backtest strategies. But as the community grew, I kept running into the same two problems from opposite sides.

**Investors** wanted access to sophisticated trading strategies but didn't have the technical skills to build them. They were stuck choosing between managing portfolios manually, paying steep fees for active management, or settling for basic robo-advisors with cookie-cutter strategies.

**Developers** were building amazing algorithms but had no way to share them. Building a trading algorithm is one thing. Building the infrastructure to deploy it (execution engines, monitoring, user management, payments, compliance) is an entirely different business. Most developers just wanted to write code, not run a fintech startup.

These two groups needed each other, but there was nothing connecting them.

## What Finterion actually is

Finterion is an open marketplace that connects algorithm creators with investors. Think of it as an app store, but for trading bots.

**If you're an investor**, you can browse algorithms created by developers from around the world, compare them using verified performance metrics like CAGR, Sharpe ratio, maximum drawdown, and volatility, and subscribe to the ones that match your goals and risk tolerance. You connect your own broker account, so your funds never leave your control. You can pause or stop any algorithm at any moment.

**If you're a developer**, you can publish your algorithms without giving away your source code. Your intellectual property stays yours. You set your own pricing (fixed subscriptions or profit-sharing) and Finterion handles the infrastructure, distribution, and monetization. You focus on building great strategies.

## The principles I won't compromise on

Building a platform where people trust you with their investment decisions is a serious responsibility. There are a few things I decided early on that I won't bend on:

**Trust through transparency.** Every algorithm on Finterion shows honest metrics. We clearly separate live trading results from backtested performance. We show maximum drawdowns, worst-case scenarios, and "what can go wrong" disclosures. No cherry-picked equity curves.

**You keep control.** Finterion never holds your assets. Your money stays in your own broker account. We execute trades on your behalf through secure API connections, and you can pull the plug at any time.

**Fair pricing.** No per-trade commissions that create misaligned incentives. Either a fixed subscription for predictable costs, or profit-sharing models that align everyone's interests.

**Code stays private.** For developers, this was non-negotiable. Your source code is never exposed or shared. We verify algorithm functionality without examining your implementation. You maintain full ownership.

## Why open source matters to this

Finterion wouldn't exist without the open-source community. The [Investing Algorithm Framework](https://github.com/coding-kitties/investing-algorithm-framework) is the foundation. It's how developers build, backtest, and deploy their strategies. Tools like [PyIndicators](https://github.com/coding-kitties/pyindicators) provide the technical analysis building blocks.

I keep these projects open source because I believe the tooling should be free and accessible. The marketplace is where creators can optionally monetize their work, but the ability to build and test strategies should never be gated.

It's also a trust signal. When the framework itself is open source, developers know exactly what they're building on. There's no black box.

## Where it's going

Right now Finterion supports crypto trading across exchanges like Binance, Coinbase, Bitvavo, and Kraken. The marketplace has algorithms ranging from swing trading to trend following to mean reversion strategies.

But the vision is bigger than crypto. The same model (an open marketplace connecting strategy creators with investors, built on transparent metrics and user control) works for any asset class. Stocks, forex, commodities. The infrastructure is being built to scale.

I'm also investing heavily in the developer experience. The recently added [blotter system](/articles/blotter-system) for realistic backtesting, better documentation, and more data providers are all aimed at making it easier to go from idea to deployed algorithm.

## The bet I'm making

The bet behind Finterion is simple: algorithmic trading shouldn't be exclusive to institutions. The tools exist. The talent exists. What's been missing is the infrastructure and marketplace to connect it all.

If you're an investor curious about automated strategies, [browse the marketplace](https://finterion.com/store). If you're a developer building trading algorithms, [check out the framework](https://github.com/coding-kitties/investing-algorithm-framework) and consider publishing on the platform.

This is just the beginning.
