## What I do

I'm a Software Engineer at **Microsoft**, where I work on software systems and agentic AI applications with a focus on multi-agent orchestration.

Outside of Microsoft, I'm building **[Finterion](https://finterion.com/)** — an open source quantitative trading platform with the goal of democratizing algorithmic strategies by connecting quant developers with investors. I also maintain open source projects under **[Coding Kitties](https://github.com/coding-kitties)**, including the [Investing Algorithm Framework](https://github.com/coding-kitties/investing-algorithm-framework) and [PyIndicators](https://github.com/coding-kitties/PyIndicators).

My work sits at the intersection of software engineering, financial markets, and quantitative research. I spend a lot of time researching quantitative trading strategies — from signal generation and backtesting methodology to portfolio construction and risk management.

## Background

I studied at the **University of Leiden** and the **University of Amsterdam**, where I developed a strong foundation in software engineering and system design. Over the years I've worked across different domains — from enterprise cloud infrastructure to fintech — but the common thread has always been building systems that are modular, testable, and built to last.

I care deeply about software craftsmanship: clean architecture, domain boundaries, separation of concerns, and making deliberate trade-offs between shipping fast and building things right.

## Why this blog exists

There's a difference between _programming_ and _software engineering_. Programming is writing code that works. Software engineering is building systems that remain maintainable, adaptable, and performant as they grow — often under constraints you didn't anticipate.

I write here to document what I learn along the way: architecture decisions in Finterion, patterns that worked (and didn't), quantitative trading research, and the general craft of building software systems. If something I've worked through saves you time or sparks an idea, that's a win.

## What I write about

- **Software Engineering** — clean architecture, system design, parallelization, and the craft of writing code that lasts
- **Quantitative Trading** — strategy research, backtesting infrastructure, and the technical side of algorithmic trading

- [**Finterion**](https://finterion.com/) - Building an quantitative trading platform, architecture decisions, and the journey of connecting quant developers with investors
